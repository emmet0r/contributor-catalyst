# Catalyst Program Expectations & Goals

## Expectations for Everyone

- Be on time to lab and all group events.
- Don't wander off by yourself.
- Have courage and be kind!
- Be respectful no inappropriate language, jokes, or comments.
- Help each other when needed.
- Be patient with one another.
- Check Slack every day (except weekends unless we have an event).
- Refer to Trello first, then ask for help when you need it.
- Be open-minded to new experiences.
- Be safe.
- Informal communication is okay, but remain professional. If you're unsure where the line is, just ask!
- No headphones during lab or group activities.
- Have fun!
- Be mindful of phone usage and other distractions during lab and group activities.
- Celebrate one another's successes and accomplishments.
- If someone is falling behind or having a hard time, offer to help them catch up.
- Don't leave people behind.
- Check in on one another.

---

## Expectations for the Lead Mentor/Instructor (Emily)

- Respond to Slack messages in a timely manner.
- Communicate clearly.
- Provide advice and professional opportunities to Catalyst participants.
- Share important and useful information.
- Be available to answer questions—there are no dumb questions!
- Help provide additional opportunities for professional growth whenever possible.
- Introduce participants to open source topics, communities, and connections that can help in the future.

---

## Expectations for Graduate Student & Alumni Mentors (Silas & Jordin)

- Be someone participants can ask questions they may not feel comfortable asking Emily.
- Respond to messages and DMs in a timely manner (phone calls/texts for urgent matters).
- Provide assistance whenever needed.
- Be a positive role model both inside and outside of the lab.
- Share lessons learned from previous years to help prevent repeated mistakes.
- Support the cohort by answering simpler questions.
- Help research solutions when the answer isn't immediately known.

---

## Expectations for Catalyst Participants

- Help each other whenever possible.
- Be respectful of fellow participants.
- Come prepared every day (laptop, charger, etc.).
- Check Slack and email regularly for updates.
- Be an active participant during co-working sessions.
- Silence devices before coming to lab.
- Connect with each other's networks and be generous with opportunities.
- Research problems before asking for assistance.
- Use this opportunity to positively impact your professional development and coding skills.
- Be open to new experiences and step outside your comfort zone to grow.
- Engage authentically. Do not rely excessively on AI unless its use has been approved.
- Treat this like an internship. Although you won't receive grades, Emily will provide feedback throughout the program, and all program requirements must be completed to receive stipends.
- Complete all activities on time and to the best of your ability.
- Learn, stay engaged, and pay attention.
- Ask for help when you need it—don't suffer in silence.
